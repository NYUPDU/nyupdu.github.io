Gov vs Opp ‚Äî what competing from each side means

Government (Gov)

Your job: propose a fair, winnable case; define terms; explain mechanism; give 2‚Äì4 warranted reasons + weighing.

Do
  • Be clear & accessible; stake comparative outcomes.
  • Explain mechanism (who acts, incentives, feasibility).
  • Keep it debatable (avoid tight/spec/truisms).
Avoid
  • Squirrely definitions or unwinnable setups.
  • Thin warrants (taglines without reasons).

Motions rounds: Gov also preps 15 minutes after motion reveal.

Opposition (Opp)

Your job: take the best ground; warrant your responses; weigh early.

Do
  • Group & answer with warrants; find mechanism gaps.
  • Flag tight/spec if needed‚Äîkeep it minimal and clear.
  • Start the weighing story early.
Avoid
  • Chasing every tag while conceding the center.

Case rounds: Opp gets 15 minutes to prep after the case statement; ask POCs during prep.

How to argue ‚Äî Claim ‚Üí Warrants ‚Üí Impacts

Every winning point has three parts: the Claim you assert, the Warrants (reasons/mechanism) that make it true, and the Impact (why it matters).

  • Claim: what you assert.
  • Warrants: why it‚Äôs true (incentives, feasibility, cause‚Üíeffect, actors).
  • Impacts: so what? who is affected and how?

Then compare: scope & magnitude, probability, timeframe, reversibility ‚Äî this is weighing.

Stems: ‚ÄúOur mechanism makes X because Y, which means Z.‚Äù ¬∑ ‚ÄúEven if they achieve X, ours wins on magnitude/probability/timeframe because Y.‚Äù

Rebuttals & Points of Order (POOs)

Rebuttals (LOR/PMR) = collapse + weigh + ballot story. No new arguments.

How to raise a POO

  1. Stand briefly: ‚ÄúPoint of order.‚Äù
  2. One line: ‚ÄúNew argument in rebuttal on [issue].‚Äù
  3. Judge rules: ‚ÄúWell taken / Not well taken‚Äù; speech resumes.

Etiquette: brief, neutral, respectful.

POO scenarios

Are these valid POOs?

POOs are primarily for procedural violations like new arguments in rebuttals or timing, not constructives like the MO.

HOW TO WIN

  • Pull through your best arguments. Collapse hard.
  • Always warrant. Explain the mechanism; no naked claims.
  • Always weigh. Why your world matters more (magnitude/probability/timeframe/reversibility).
  • Ballot story. ‚ÄúJudge, vote Gov/Opp because‚Ä¶‚Äù in one sentence.

  • Abbreviate; number live arguments (G1/O1‚Ä¶) for clean tracking.

Partnering 101

Resource management

The 2-minute pact

  • Assign: master flow keeper, timekeeper, POO watcher, case notes.
  • Agree: max 2 passed notes to speaker (missing warrant; weighing line).
  • Set simple signals (speed, finish, move on).

During speeches

  • Speaker talks; partner flows and stars warrants/impacts.
  • Track protected/grace time; mark clash & drops.
  • When roles switch, swap focus but keep one master flow.

15-minute prep: Case rounds ‚Üí Opp only (after hearing case). Motions ‚Üí both teams (after motion). POCs during prep clarify definitions/mechanism.

Building a Case

Fair, simple, winnable

  • Resolution: ‚ÄúTHBT ‚Ä¶‚Äù (clear, accessible).
  • Definitions: key terms only; keep ground fair.
  • Actors & Mechanism: who acts, why it works (incentives, feasibility).
  • Reasons (2‚Äì4): each with warrants ‚Üí impact; then weigh.
  • Comparative world: how your world is better.
  • Guardrails: avoid tight/spec/truisms; keep it debatable.

POIs

  • Allowed after 1:00 and before the last 1:00 of constructives.
  • Optional to offer and optional to accept ‚Äî taking one is tactical/courtesy, not mandatory.
  • Keep them brief and polite; rebuttals do not take POIs.
