APDA in about 60 seconds

  • 2v2: Government (PM, MG) vs Opposition (LO, MO).
  • Speech order & times (grace baked in): 7:30 · 8:30 · 8:30 · 8:30 · 4:30 · 5:30.
  • Case rounds: Gov writes & presents the case; Opp gets 15 minutes to prep after hearing it.
  • Motions rounds: no one knows beforehand; both teams get 15 minutes to prep from motion reveal.
  • POCs (points of clarification) happen right after the case statement to clarify defs/mechanism.
  • Judges reward: clear claims, strong warrants, and explicit weighing.
Quick check: tap the speeches in the correct order.

Gov vs Opp — what competing from each side means

Government (PM · MG)You write the case
  • Your job: propose a clear, fair case; define terms; explain mechanism; give 2–4 warranted reasons + weighing.
  • Do: be accessible; compare worlds; keep it debatable.
  • Avoid: squirrely defs; unwinnable/truistic/tight cases.
  • Prep reality: in motions, you also take 15 minutes after the motion.
Opposition (LO · MO)You prep 15 after hearing the case
  • Your job: take the best ground; answer with warrants; start weighing early.
  • Do: group answers; expose mechanism gaps; flag tight/spec if needed.
  • Avoid: chasing every tag; conceding the center.
  • POCs: ask immediately after the case statement to clarify defs/mechanism.

How to argue: Claim → Warrants → Impacts

Warranting is the #1 novice hurdle. A claim without warrants doesn’t land.

  • Claim — what you assert.
  • Warrants — the reasons/mechanism making the claim true (incentives, feasibility, causal link).
  • Impacts — why it matters (who is affected and how).

Level up with magnitude, probability, timeframe, reversibility — then weigh vs their best impact.

What’s missing?
“Ban X → fewer harms.”

Stems: “Our mechanism makes X happen because Y, which means Z.” · “Even if they achieve X, ours wins on mag/prob/time because Y.”

Roles (one open at a time)

Prime Minister (PM) 7:30
  • Define terms; set a fair, clear case.
  • Explain mechanism (who/what/how).
  • Give 2–4 warranted reasons; begin weighing.
Leader of Opposition (LO) 8:30
  • Frame the round and stance.
  • Group & answer with warrants.
  • Start your weighing story.
Member of Government (MG) 8:30
  • Defend the center with warrants.
  • Answer their best lines well.
  • Collapse; set PMR’s weighing.
Member of Opposition (MO) 8:30
  • Extend Opp’s best offense.
  • Turn/mitigate Gov benefits.
  • Set LOR voters with weighing.

Rebuttals & Points of Order (POOs)

  • LOR / PMR: collapse to the winning path, weigh explicitly, and give a ballot story.
  • No new arguments in rebuttals (respond/extend; don’t introduce fresh offense).
  • POO: stand briefly, say “Point of order,” state the issue in one line (“New argument in rebuttal on [issue]”). Judge rules “well taken / not well taken.” Be brief, neutral, respectful.
Valid POO? “PMR introduces a fresh impact on a new line.”
Valid POO? “LOR misquotes MG but doesn’t add a new claim.”
Point of order: new argument in rebuttal on [issue].

HOW TO WIN

  • Pull through your best arguments — collapse hard; don’t chase every tag.
  • Always warrant (mechanism) and always weigh (why yours matters more).
  • Force them to disprove or out-weigh you — make that hard.
  • Ballot story: “Judge, vote Gov/Opp because…” (1–2 sentences).

Flowing & Clash

  • Flow the essentials: Claim → 1–2 Warrants → Impact per line; abbreviate; number live arguments (G1/O1…).
  • Clash: mark where arguments meet.
  • Drops: say it aloud in rebuttals (“They dropped X; extend and weigh.”).
  • Partner not speaking keeps the master flow and stars warrants/impacts to pull through.
Where’s the drop? G1 → O1 (answers magnitude) → MG (answers timeframe) → MO (…)

Partnering 101 (resource management)

Before round (2-minute pact)

  • Assign: master flow keeper, timekeeper, POO watcher, case notes.
  • Agree to pass at most two notes to the speaker: missing warrant; weighing line.
  • Pick simple signals (finish point · move on · time cues).

15-minute prep

  • Case round: Gov already prepped; Opp gets 15 after hearing the case.
  • Motions: both teams get 15 after motion reveal.
  • POCs: ask right after the case statement to clarify defs/mechanism.

During speeches

  • Speaker talks; partner flows and stars warrants/impacts to pull through.
  • Track protected/grace; flag clash and drops.
  • In rebuttals: flow only what you’re collapsing to (and their answers to it).

How much to flow?

  • Don’t write everything — capture the warranted, weighable lines.
  • Use arrows (→) and short actor names; keep it readable.

Building a Case (fair, simple, winnable)

  • Resolution (“THBT…”), key definitions, actors & mechanism.
  • 2–4 warranted reasons + impacts; compare worlds explicitly.
  • Keep it debatable (avoid tight/spec); be clear and accessible.

POIs (tiny note)

Allowed after 1:00 and before the last 1:00 of constructives. Optional in many rounds — keep them concise and polite. Rebuttals don’t take POIs.

Glossary (brief)

Flow: notes tracing arguments across speeches.
POO: “Point of Order”; flags new-in-rebuttal / time issues; judge rules.
Weighing: compare worlds by magnitude, probability, timeframe, reversibility.
Framework: what matters and why (the lens for the round).
Turn: make their argument support your side.
Extension: carry an argument forward with deeper warrants.
Collapse: focus on the few arguments that win.
Tight: unbeatable Gov case; Opp can call it tight.
Spec: specific knowledge not in POCs/common knowledge; not evaluated.
RFD: Reason for Decision; judge’s explanation.
Clash: where arguments directly meet.
Drop: a line left unanswered; say it aloud and weigh it.
