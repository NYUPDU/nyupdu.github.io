About the Parliamentary Debate Union

Who We Are

The Parliamentary Debate Union (PDU) at New York University is a student-run club devoted to competitive debate, intellectual growth, and team camaraderie. We represent NYU on the American Parliamentary Debate Association (APDA) circuit, a collegiate league of debate teams from universities across the East Coast and beyond.

Each weekend, we send teams to tournaments at colleges like Yale, Princeton, and Harvard, fully funded and supported. PDU covers all travel expenses for competing members, including transportation, registration, and lodging.

Beyond competition, PDU offers weekly training workshops, practice debates, and mentorship. We welcome everyone from seasoned debaters to complete beginners and provide the tools, strategies, and community to help every member succeed.

Debate with us, grow with us, and find your voice at NYU.

What is APDA?

The American Parliamentary Debate Association (APDA) is a collegiate debate league that emphasizes clear analysis and persuasive speaking over reading evidence at high speed. Rounds feature two-person teams on each side: the Government proposes a case and the Opposition responds.

Teams & Speaker Roles

  • Government (Gov): Prime Minister (PM) and Member of Government (MG)
  • Opposition (Opp): Leader of the Opposition (LO) and Member of the Opposition (MO)

Speech Order & Times

PMC 7:00 LOC 8:00 MG 8:00 MO 8:00 LOR 4:00 PMR 5:00

Constructive speeches allow Points of Information (POIs) except in the first and last minute (“protected time”). Rebuttals do not take POIs.

How Rounds Are Judged

  • Judges weigh comparative persuasion: which side better supports and compares its claims.
  • Clarity, structure, and weighing matter more than quoted evidence.
  • Gov presents a clear case in the PMC; Opp refutes and offers counter-analysis.

Meetings & Attendance

Schedule: Mondays & Wednesdays, 7:00–9:00 PM (twice weekly)

Monday — Lesson Meeting

Led by the Education Officer and focused on skill-building.
Flow: Lecture → Engagement → Practice Rounds

  • Lecture: A focused lesson on a debate skill or technique.
  • Engagement: Short activities to check understanding.
  • Practice Rounds: Full simulated debates to apply the lesson.

Wednesday — Training Meeting

Open work time for reps, case-building, and tournament prep.
Flow: Practice Round → Workshopping → Feedback

  • Practice Round: A full simulated debate round.
  • Workshopping: Members refine cases with senior members and the board.
  • Feedback: Real-time notes from Education and Leadership on round and workshop performance.

Tournament Eligibility (Global Policy)

To be considered for a tournament, you must meet these minimums in the three weeks before the interest form deadline:

  • 1 Monday Lesson Meeting
  • 2 Wednesday Training Meetings

Attending more than the minimum improves your chances when seats are limited.

Voting Eligibility

To vote in Board elections, you must attend at least 60% of meetings in the semester.

Tracking & Accountability

At the start of each meeting, check in on the tablet to record your attendance. The attendance sheet is visible to members so you can track your own record and standing. Any disputes should be filed with the Operations Director.
